# Leave Policy — WIN Holding Group (Egypt)
**Tier:** Public | **Owner:** HR | **Version:** 2.0 | **Review:** Annual  
**Source:** WIN Holding Group Leaves Policy Ref: HR/BTE 001/7-2025, Effective 1 August 2025  
**Supersedes:** Version 1.0 (generic Labour Law draft)

This policy applies to all full-time employees who have passed the probationary period unless otherwise stated. Leave entitlements derive from Egyptian Labour Law No. 12 of 2003 and the WIN Holding Group internal policy. Where the company provides more generous terms than statutory minimums, the company terms apply. Where the law provides more generous terms than this policy, the law prevails.

---

## 1. Eligibility

Leave under this policy requires:
- Full-time employment status
- Direct manager approval
- Completion of the probationary period (except sick leave and funeral leave, which apply from day one)

Eligibility may be reviewed and adjusted by Department Heads in collaboration with HR and Management if misuse is detected or if workflow and productivity are impacted.

---

## 2. Annual (Vacation) Leave

### 2.1 Entitlement
- **First hiring year:** 15 working days (pro-rated from hire date to December 31).
- **Standard entitlement:** 21 working days per year, split as **14 regular days + 7 casual days**.
- **Enhanced entitlement:** 30 working days per year (23 regular + 7 casual) if the employee is **aged 50 or above** OR has **10 or more years of Social Insurance contributions**.
- The leave year runs January to December. First-year allocation is pro-rated based on the hire date.
- Annual leave is granted retroactively after the probationary period.

### 2.2 Casual Leave Sub-Quota
Of the 21-day (or 30-day) annual entitlement, **7 days are designated as casual leave**. Casual leave is limited to a **maximum of 2 consecutive days** per request. The remaining days are regular annual leave.

### 2.3 Advance Notice
- Requests of **2–3 days:** minimum **24 hours** (1 working day) advance notice.
- Requests exceeding **3 consecutive days:** minimum **7 working days** advance notice.
- Requests submitted with shorter notice may be declined at the manager's discretion.

### 2.4 Carry-Over
- A maximum of **5 days** of unused annual leave may be carried forward to the following year.
- Carried-over days must be used in **Q1 (January–March)** of the following year. Any unused carry-over expires on March 31.
- Carry-over beyond 5 days is not permitted.

### 2.5 Concurrent Absence Cap (Soft Threshold)
No more than **25% of any department or team** may be on approved leave simultaneously. This threshold is a default; HR managers may grant exceptions with documented operational justification. Exceptions are logged.

### 2.6 Approval Process
Submit your request via the HR system. Your direct manager approves or declines. HR Operations verifies the request against leave records and updates the HRIS (Odoo). The system notifies you of the decision.

### 2.7 Leave Beyond Available Balance
If an employee submits a leave request with insufficient balance, the absence will be treated as **Leave Without Pay**. The corresponding salary deduction will be applied in the next payroll cycle.

### 2.8 Leave During Probation
Leave taken during the probationary period will be **deducted from salary**. Annual leave balance is granted retroactively after probation completion.

### 2.9 Recall from Leave
In exceptional operational circumstances, the company may recall an employee from approved annual leave. Reasonable costs incurred will be reimbursed and the leave rescheduled.

### 2.10 Leave Balance on Termination
On resignation or termination, any remaining leave balance will be **paid out to the employee** at their daily rate based on base salary. Negative balances (leave taken in advance) are deducted from final pay.

## 3. Sick Leave

### 3.1 Entitlement
Sick leave is calculated per the Egyptian Labor Law:
- First 3 months: **full pay**
- Next 3 months: **75% of pay**
- Following months: as per Labour Law provisions and internal business needs

### 3.2 Medical Certificate Requirement
Sick leave must be supported by a **detailed medical report with doctor recommendations from the company's designated Medical Service Provider network**. The report must be submitted to the HR team as an attachment. Certificates from physicians outside the company network may not be accepted without HR Director approval.

### 3.3 HARD RULE — Sick Leave With Valid Certificate Cannot Be Rejected
**A request to reject sick leave that is supported by a valid medical certificate from the company's medical service provider network will be blocked by the system.**
This is a non-negotiable legal obligation under Egyptian Labour Law. If there is a dispute about the validity of the certificate, the correct path is referral to the company-designated physician — not rejection of the leave.

### 3.4 Notification
For unplanned sick leave, notify your manager or HR before your scheduled start time. If incapacitated, a family member may notify on your behalf.

## 4. Marriage Leave

- **Service requirement:** Minimum **1 year** of continuous service at the company.
- **Entitlement:** **5 working days** paid leave, covered by the company.
- **Frequency:** **Once** during the entire period of service at the company.
- Any additional days beyond 5 will be deducted from the employee's annual vacation balance.

---

## 5. Hajj Leave

- **Service requirement:** The employee must have been working for the company for more than **5 consecutive years** on a full-time basis.
- **Entitlement:** **One month** (30 calendar days) to perform a pilgrimage to Mecca or Jerusalem.
- **Frequency:** **Once** in the total period of work at the company.
- The employee must provide documentation of pilgrimage registration and agree dates with the manager in advance.

---

## 6. Umrah Leave

- **Service requirement:** Minimum **1 year** of continuous employment.
- **Entitlement:** **5 working days** paid leave, covered by the company.
- **Frequency:** **Once** during the service period.

---

## 7. Military Service Summon

- Leave is provided in accordance with the requirements of law, up to the duration specified in the **formal letter received from the military authority**.
- **Full paid leave** during the period of summons.
- The formal military letter must be submitted to HR before the leave commences.
- Employment rights are preserved; the employee has the right to return to their role on completion.

---

## 8. Educational Leave

- The **exam schedule must be shared with HR and the direct manager** in advance.
- Leave is restricted to the **days of exams only** — not the full study period.
- There is no fixed annual day cap; entitlement is per-exam as documented.
- Enrolment confirmation and exam timetable must be provided to HR.

---

## 9. Funeral Leave

### 9.1 First Degree Relatives
**3 working days** paid leave for the death of a first-degree relative:
- Father, Mother, Son, Daughter, Husband, Wife

### 9.2 Second Degree Relatives
**1 working day** paid leave for the death of a second-degree relative:
- Grandfather, Grandmother, Brother, Sister, Grandsons

### 9.3 Additional Leave
Any additional days beyond the entitlement will be deducted from annual leave balance or treated as unpaid leave if the balance is exhausted.

---

## 10. Maternity Leave

- **Service requirement:** Minimum **1 year** of continuous service at the company.
- **Entitlement:** **4 months** (approximately 120 calendar days) at **full pay**.
- **Frequency:** Eligible for a maximum of **3 times** during the entire period of service at the company.
- Return-to-work protection applies per Egyptian Labour Law — the employee may not be dismissed during maternity leave.
- Breastfeeding breaks: two 30-minute breaks per day (or one consolidated hour) for up to 24 months from date of birth, per Labour Law Article 93.

---

## 11. Paternity Leave

- **Entitlement:** **1 working day** paid leave, to be taken on the **delivery/surgery day only**.
- **Frequency:** Eligible for a maximum of **3 times** during the entire service period.
- Supporting documentation (hospital record or birth certificate) is required.

---

## 12. Compensatory Off Leave

- Employees who work at least **4 hours** on an official holiday or weekend are entitled to **1 compensatory day off**.
- The compensatory day is **added to the vacation balance**.
- Subject to **prior approval by the Direct Manager** before working the holiday/weekend.

---

## 13. Work From Home (WFH)

### 13.1 Eligibility
WFH is available to roles where the nature of work permits remote delivery, as determined by the department head and HR.

### 13.2 Frequency Limits
Up to **2 days per week** by default. Limits vary by department.

### 13.3 Approval
WFH days must be requested and approved in advance. Ad hoc WFH without approval may be treated as absent.

---

## 14. Unpaid Leave

Available for personal reasons after exhausting paid entitlements. Approval is at HR's discretion and subject to operational requirements. The period does not count toward service for annual leave accrual or gratuity calculation.

---

## 15. Public Holidays

- The company observes all national and religious public holidays declared by the Egyptian government.
- The annual public holiday list is published by HR in December for the following year.
- Working on a public holiday: entitled to a compensatory day off (see §12) or compensation at 170% of daily rate, at the employee's election.

---

## 16. Guidelines and Conditions

1. **Approval Requirement:** All leave is subject to managerial approval and may be declined based on business needs. Emergency or unplanned leave must be communicated as early as possible.
2. **Leave Balance Check:** Employees should verify their available balance before applying. Leave beyond the available balance is treated as Leave Without Pay.
3. **Unauthorized Absence:** Absence without proper communication or approval is treated as unauthorized and may result in disciplinary action. 7+ consecutive days or 20+ non-consecutive days in a year is grounds for summary dismissal per Labour Law Article 61.
4. **Leave During Notice Period:** Taking leave during the notice period requires **special approval** and is usually discouraged.
5. **Combination of Leaves:** Combining multiple leave types (e.g., sick + annual) in one continuous absence requires **advance approval**.
6. **Monitoring:** Department Heads, in collaboration with HR and Management, may modify leave eligibility or privileges at any time if misuse is detected or if workflow and productivity are impacted.

---

## 17. Process Flow

1. Employee submits leave request via the HR system (or to Department Head, copying HR)
2. Department Head / Direct Manager approves or declines the request
3. Approved request is sent to the HR portal for records
4. HR Operations verifies the request against leave records and updates Odoo / HRIS
