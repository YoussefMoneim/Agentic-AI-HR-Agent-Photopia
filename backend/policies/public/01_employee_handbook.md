# Employee Handbook
**Tier:** Public | **Owner:** HR | **Version:** 1.0 | **Review:** Annual

---

## 1. Welcome and Company Overview

This handbook sets out the terms and conditions of your employment and the standards Fotopia expects of all employees. It supplements your employment contract; in any conflict, your contract prevails for individual terms, and applicable Egyptian law prevails over both.

**Our values:** Integrity — we do what we say and say what we mean. Innovation — we build things that matter, not things that are easy. Trust — our clients and colleagues depend on us; we earn that every day. Inclusion — the best ideas come from diverse perspectives. Accountability — we own our outcomes, good and bad.

---

## 1A. Confidentiality and NDA

All employees are required to sign a Non-Disclosure Agreement (NDA) on or before their first day. The NDA governs your obligations regarding Fotopia's confidential information, client data, and intellectual property. Your obligations under the NDA survive the termination of your employment. Refer to the Code of Conduct (Section 3) for the full confidentiality policy.

---

## 2. Employment Categories

**Full-time:** Standard contracted hours, full benefits entitlement.  
**Part-time:** Contracted for fewer than standard hours; benefits pro-rated proportionally.  
**Probationary:** Any new employee in their first three months. Full legal protections apply. Maximum probationary period is three months under Egyptian Labour Law Article 75. Cannot be extended or repeated for the same role.  
**Fixed-term contract:** Defined start and end date. Automatically expires unless renewed in writing before expiry.

---

## 3. Working Hours

### 3.1 Standard Hours
The standard working week is 8 hours per day, 48 hours per week, in accordance with Egyptian Labour Law Article 81. Actual scheduled hours are set per department and communicated at hire.

### 3.2 Ramadan Hours
During the holy month of Ramadan, working hours for Muslim employees are reduced by two hours per day, making the standard Ramadan day 6 hours. Schedules are issued by HR before Ramadan begins each year.

### 3.3 Shift Work
Employees on shift rotations receive their schedules at least two weeks in advance. Shift changes require five working days' notice except in operational emergencies.

### 3.4 Overtime
Overtime requires prior written authorisation from your line manager. Unauthorised overtime may not be compensated. Rates: 135% of standard hourly rate for weekday overtime; 170% for Fridays and public holidays, per Labour Law Article 82. Maximum overtime is **144 hours** per year.

---

## 4. Attendance and Punctuality

### 4.1 Attendance Expectation
You are expected to be present and ready to work at your scheduled start time. Consistent punctuality is a condition of continued employment.

### 4.2 Absence Notification
If you are unable to attend work, you must notify your line manager or HR by phone or approved messaging channel **before** your scheduled start time, or as soon as is practically possible. Email alone is not sufficient for unplanned absences.

### 4.3 Tardiness
Arriving more than 15 minutes late constitutes tardiness and will be recorded. Three instances of tardiness within a calendar month may result in a formal discussion with your manager.

### 4.4 Unauthorised Absence
Absence without approval or valid notification is unauthorised. Continuous unauthorised absence of 7 or more days, or 20 or more non-consecutive days in a calendar year, are grounds for summary dismissal under Labour Law Article 61.

---

## 5. Probationary Period

Your first three months of employment are a probationary period. During this time, you and Fotopia assess mutual fit. Your manager will hold a formal review at the 6-week and 12-week marks. At the end of the probation, your manager will either confirm your employment, extend the review in exceptional circumstances, or — if performance or conduct standards have not been met — end your employment. All statutory rights apply during probation.

---

## 6. Employment Contracts

Your employment contract is a legally binding document. Any changes to its terms require written agreement from both you and an authorised Fotopia signatory. Read it carefully before signing. Keep a copy.

---

## 7. Dress Code

Fotopia operates a business-professional dress code. Dress should be clean, appropriate for a professional environment, and respectful of cultural norms. Client-facing roles may have additional requirements communicated by your manager. Where safety requires specific attire (e.g. PPE), that requirement overrides personal preference.

---

## 8. Company Assets and Facilities

Company equipment (laptops, phones, access cards) is provided for work purposes. You are responsible for their care and must return all assets on your last day of employment. Misuse or damage through negligence may result in deductions from final pay to the extent permitted by law.

## 9. Workplace Facilities

Fotopia provides: a clean, safe working environment; prayer facilities where available; kitchen and break areas; first-aid supplies. Employees are expected to respect shared spaces and leave them clean and usable for colleagues.

## 10. Personal Data and Privacy

Fotopia collects and holds personal data about employees to manage the employment relationship. This is governed by Egypt's Personal Data Protection Law (Law No. 151 of 2020). You have the right to access, correct, and in some circumstances request deletion of your personal data. For queries, contact HR. The full data protection policy is available in the HR system.
